The quiet repricing: why midcap earnings are rewriting the playbook

For two years the story was megacap dominance. A new quarter of filings suggests a different kind of discipline is winning — and the operators who understand it already look different on the inside.

Sarah Mendel
8 minute read Today, 09:14
✦

In the autumn of 2025, a handful of small-cap filings began telling a story the rest of the market had stopped listening for. The pattern was unglamorous at first: pricing power, disciplined working-capital cycles, and CFOs who had learned to say no.

It took another two quarters before anyone in New York put a name to what was happening. By then the repricing was already underway, and the operators who understood it were doing something that, on paper, looked almost conservative — something that would have seemed out of place in 2023.

“

The companies that have done best this quarter are the ones that stopped confusing velocity with progress.

Anna Choi · CFO, Vertices Capital

That sentence — in a routine analyst call on a Wednesday in March — is what the rest of this essay is about.
