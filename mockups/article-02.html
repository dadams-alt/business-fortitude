The scale-up floor, day two.

Four thousand founders, a pitch stage promising £3,000 in coaching to the winners, and a government-backed AI fund the whole room is quietly pricing into plans. Our live notebook from the Business Design Centre as Britain's growth stage meets in one room.

Rachel Okonkwo
Startups correspondent · Islington
23 Apr 2026 · 08:42 · Updated 14:18 BST
In this story: SCALE London SCALE Ventures Liz Barclay Dwain Reid UK Business Angels Association Sovereign AI Unit

The room does a particular thing in its second hour. The polite pitch-practice energy of the opening dissipates, the coffees land, and founders start talking to each other about the numbers they didn't put on the slides. That's SCALE at its best. It is also, this year, the first proper read of whether the government's scale-up posture since Easter has actually moved behaviour on the ground.

More than four thousand scale-up leaders are at the Business Design Centre in Islington for the two-day run, according to organisers, with roughly seven in ten attendees being founders or C-suite. That ratio matters. It is not a sponsor conference wearing a founder badge. The floor is working.

"Everyone in this room has done the ten-million number. The question is whether the rails under the next ten million have actually improved." Female founder, B2B SaaS · On the floor, day one

What the day looks like

The day-one pitch competition, judged by former small-business commissioner Liz Barclay, spotlighted female founders; day two, headed by Dwain Reid, gives the stage to founders from under-represented communities. Two winners walk away with a package including strategic coaching worth £3,000 from SCALE Ventures and its partners.

Three themes from our rounds this morning. First, the Sovereign AI Unit's £500m remit, announced on 16 April, is being priced into plans more aggressively than we expected. Founders who are nowhere near the initial seven-company cohort are still structuring UK-first hiring assumptions around the compute-access track. Second, the 6 April tax-relief expansion of EMI, EIS and VCT is changing what Series A-to-B rounds look like, and advisers on the floor are already rewriting option-grant templates. Third, the day-one employment rights that came in the same week are taking up more CFO time than any of the AI conversations.

Day-two update log

14:18 BST
Dwain Reid's panel names three finalists for the under-represented founders prize

A Manchester-based MedTech, a Leeds agri-AI platform, and a London-based fintech focused on the self-employed. Winner announced at 17:30.

12:40 BST
BVCA and BBB panel: "Mid-market capital is not the problem. Sequencing is."

British Business Bank's head of growth finance and a BVCA delegate agree that capacity exists at Series B-to-C, but that founders are signing term sheets in the wrong order. "The tax-advantaged pool has just expanded. Use it first, not last."

11:05 BST
Yesterday's winner: Brighton-based HR platform wins the female founder prize

The company, which serves sub-50-headcount employers, said the £3,000 coaching package was "the most practical thing the ecosystem has given us in two years."

09:42 BST
Keynote: Technology Secretary addresses the hall on the Sovereign AI rollout

Liz Kendall tells founders the Unit will make a second cohort announcement "before the summer recess". No ambiguity on the direction of travel.

08:15 BST
Doors open. Day-two badges are through in twelve minutes.

Faster than day one. Organisers credit the move of the badge desk to the north side of the atrium.

Key takeaways

  • Seven in ten of the 4,000+ attendees are founders or C-suite. The ratio matters. It is a working floor, not a sponsor floor.
  • The Sovereign AI Unit's £500m remit is already being priced into UK-first hiring assumptions, even by founders outside the cohort.
  • Advisers on the floor are rewriting option-grant templates this week to reflect the 6 April EMI expansion.
  • Day-one employment rights are taking up more CFO-level time at SCALE than any of the AI-strategy conversations.
  • A second Sovereign AI cohort is flagged by the Technology Secretary for "before the summer recess".

The room's mood, in one sentence

The floor is less giddy than last year and more pragmatic than the year before that. Founders are planning. They are doing arithmetic. They are not pitching each other. That is a healthier room than it looks from the outside, and a more useful one than the glossier year would suggest. Business Fortitude will continue updating through the close of day two.
