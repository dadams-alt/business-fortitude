Profile

Ashley Steel's second act began the day she stopped signing off audits and started sitting across the table asking what else had been left out. After two decades at KPMG — rising to vice-chair before stepping out in 2013 — she has assembled one of the most deliberate non-executive portfolios in London.

Operators describe her method as "pre-rehearsed discomfort": she tells new boards, publicly, the three questions she will insist on answering within her first year. If a CEO flinches at any of them, she says so. One chair describes her contribution as "the single vote I know I can't predict, and the one I check my thinking against first."

"The job is not to avoid hard questions. The job is to make sure the wrong ones don't get asked first."
Ashley Steel · FT Non-Executive Directors' Award, 2023

Steel has been particularly influential at Games Workshop, where her audit-committee review of the Amazon licensing arrangement is credited with tightening disclosure around royalty revenue recognition — a detail the sell-side later flagged as material.

Her style is sometimes described as exacting. She is unapologetic about it: "If a committee's uncomfortable, it's probably because the business is, too."
